Q.¶

What is an ETS(A,A,A) model?

Answer.¶

The ETS(A,A,A) model is a state space time series model where all three componenets of a series (residual, trend, and seasonal components) undergo an additive temporal change.

Q. Post Mortem¶

Which strategy is better? BU or TD?

How would you quantitatively evaluate which strategy performed better? (This is NOT a trivial question. I suggest you do research.)

Answer.¶

It depends on which one we are forecasting. Citing (Athanasopoulos, Ahmed, & Hyndman, 2009), FPP3 sites that "Because historical proportions used for disaggregation do not take account of how those proportions may change over time, top-down approaches based on historical proportions tend to produce less accurate forecasts at lower levels of the hierarchy than bottom-up approaches." Following this logic may imply that when forecasting higher levels using the bottom-up approaches, errors at each level add up, resulting to greater error at higher levels.

For this problem we can observe by comparing the errors shown below that the top-down approach performed better when forecasting the total aggregated data. While it generally loses to the bottom-up approach on the per state forecasting. Shown are the proportions where the top-down approaches had greater errors than the bottom-up approach at each level. Further, code below shows that at the Total, the top-down approach had less errors on all three metrics than the bottom-up.
